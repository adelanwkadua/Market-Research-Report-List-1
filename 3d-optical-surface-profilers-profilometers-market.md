<p><p>3D optical surface profilers, or profilometers, are precision instruments used to measure surface topography at a microscopic level. The white light interference method utilizes the interference of light waves to achieve high-resolution measurements across various surfaces. Confocal technology, on the other hand, employs focused laser beams to capture detailed surface features, allowing for greater depth resolution. Both methods are essential in sectors like materials science, electronics, and biology, where accurate surface characterization is critical for quality control and research.</p></p>
