<p><p>The commercial aircraft gas turbine engine market primarily consists of high-bypass and low-bypass types. High-bypass engines are characterized by a large volume of air bypassing the engine core, leading to greater fuel efficiency and reduced noise, making them ideal for long-haul flights. In contrast, low-bypass engines have a smaller bypass ratio, delivering higher thrust, making them suitable for military applications and short-haul flights. Both types cater to different operational requirements, influencing design and performance in commercial aviation.</p></p>
