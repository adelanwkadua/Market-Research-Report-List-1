<p><p>Silicone elastomers are widely used in the medical market due to their biocompatibility and flexibility. The key types include Room Temperature Vulcanizing (RTV) silicone, which cures at room temperature, making it ideal for mold-making and applications that require quick setup. High-Temperature Vulcanizing (HTV) silicone, on the other hand, is cured using heat, offering enhanced mechanical properties and stability in extreme conditions. Both types are essential for creating durable medical devices, seals, and components that meet stringent health standards.</p></p>
