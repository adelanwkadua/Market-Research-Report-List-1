<p><p>The MRI Safe Pacemaker market consists of various types designed to be compatible with MRI procedures. Leadless pacemakers are miniaturized devices implanted directly in the heart, eliminating leads. Single-chamber pacemakers control one heart chamber, while dual-chamber pacemakers regulate both the atrium and ventricle. Biventricular pacemakers, often used in heart failure, synchronize contractions across both heart sides. Each type supports patients who require pacing while allowing access to MRI diagnostics, enhancing treatment options and safety.</p></p>
