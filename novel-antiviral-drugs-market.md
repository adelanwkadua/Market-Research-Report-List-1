<p><p>The novel antiviral drugs market is categorized into several types: Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors block viral replication by targeting the enzyme responsible for converting viral RNA into DNA. Protease Inhibitors disrupt the processing of viral proteins, hindering the assembly of new virus particles. Fusion Inhibitors prevent viruses from entering host cells by blocking fusion with cell membranes. Immune System Modulators enhance the body's immune response against viral infections. Other market segments may include compounds with novel mechanisms or those targeting specific viral strains.</p></p>
